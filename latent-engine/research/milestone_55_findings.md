# Milestone 55 Research Findings
# Portfolio Optimization Engine

## Abstract

Milestone 55 transformed the PIA Latent Engine from a predictive engineering intelligence platform into a prescriptive decision-support system by introducing deterministic portfolio optimization.

Previous milestones could identify risks, forecast future states, and evaluate hypothetical scenarios. However, they lacked the ability to determine the optimal combination of engineering interventions under practical constraints.

This milestone bridges that gap.

---

# Research Motivation

Engineering organizations rarely execute a single intervention.

Instead, they must decide:

- Which actions should be prioritized?
- Which combination yields maximum benefit?
- What is the expected engineering cost?
- Which interventions produce the highest ROI?

Portfolio Optimization addresses these questions.

---

# Research Questions

RQ1:
Can engineering recommendations be optimized mathematically rather than ranked heuristically?

RQ2:
Can deterministic optimization improve recommendation quality without sacrificing explainability?

RQ3:
Can counterfactual simulation outputs be transformed into actionable intervention portfolios?

---

# Major Findings

## 1. Counterfactuals Alone Are Not Decisions

Simulation produces possible futures.

Optimization selects the most valuable future.

Simulation answers:

"What happens if..."

Optimization answers:

"What should we choose?"

---

## 2. Portfolio Selection Outperforms Individual Ranking

Ranking interventions independently often produces suboptimal engineering outcomes.

Selecting intervention portfolios provides:

- higher expected organizational health
- better bus factor improvement
- lower engineering cost
- improved resource utilization

---

## 3. Deterministic Optimization Improves Explainability

Unlike stochastic optimization, deterministic algorithms provide:

- reproducible recommendations
- stable executive reports
- repeatable benchmarking
- easier debugging

---

## 4. Simulation Provides Rich Optimization Inputs

Simulation exposes:

- localized health changes
- ownership redistribution
- projected risk reduction
- successor readiness

These significantly improve optimization quality.

# Algorithmic Observations

Greedy optimization is fast and suitable for real-time execution.

Knapsack optimization provides better intervention combinations under engineering budget constraints.

Future work should investigate:

- Pareto optimization
- Multi-objective optimization
- Evolutionary optimization

---

# Remaining Gaps

Current optimization assumes independent interventions.

Future research should support:

- intervention dependencies
- temporal constraints
- team availability
- organizational scheduling

---

# Conclusion

Portfolio Optimization completes the transition from predictive analytics to prescriptive engineering intelligence.

The platform no longer predicts organizational futures—it recommends the optimal engineering actions required to improve them.
