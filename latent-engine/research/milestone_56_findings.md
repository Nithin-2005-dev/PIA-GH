# Milestone 56 Research Findings
# Causal Intelligence & Root Cause Analysis

## Abstract

Milestone 56 introduced deterministic Causal Intelligence into the PIA Latent Engine, enabling the platform to explain organizational risks through evidence-backed causal reasoning.

The milestone addresses one of the largest limitations of predictive systems: prediction without explanation.

---

# Research Motivation

Engineering leaders rarely accept recommendations without understanding:

- Why is the recommendation necessary?
- What caused the current organizational state?
- Which evidence supports the conclusion?
- Which intervention addresses the root cause rather than the symptom?

M56 introduces deterministic causal reasoning to answer these questions.

---

# Research Questions

RQ1:
Can deterministic causal reasoning improve engineering decision explainability?

RQ2:
Can causal mechanisms be represented without introducing probabilistic uncertainty?

RQ3:
Can engineering recommendations become fully traceable back to observations?

---

# Major Findings

## 1. Causality Improves Executive Trust

Predictions explain future outcomes.

Causality explains why those outcomes occur.

Executive users consistently require causal explanations before accepting recommendations.

---

## 2. Ontology-Based Reasoning Scales Better Than Hardcoded Rules

Introducing a Causal Ontology allows:

- semantic reasoning
- reusable mechanisms
- extensible rule providers
- improved maintainability

---

## 3. Semantic Overlay Is Superior To A Separate Graph

Rather than constructing an independent causal graph, attaching causal semantics to the existing Knowledge Graph reduces duplication while preserving consistency.

---

## 4. Confidence Should Be Decomposed

A single confidence value hides uncertainty sources.

Separating confidence into:

- Evidence
- Rule
- Propagation
- Overall

produces significantly better explainability.

---

## 5. Correlation Must Never Be Reported As Causation

The hypothesis engine rejects unsupported explanations.

A causal explanation requires:

- supporting evidence
- temporal precedence
- activated causal rule
- sufficient confidence
