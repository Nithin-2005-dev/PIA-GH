# Milestone 11 Findings

## Observation 1

Risk identification naturally leads to succession planning.

Once a system can identify ownership concentration, the next logical question becomes:

Who should take over?

Successor recommendation provides a direct response.

---

## Observation 2

Successor planning is fundamentally an ownership problem.

Ownership distributions contain enough information to generate useful initial successor recommendations.

Future versions may incorporate additional signals.

---

## Observation 3

Ownership levels should not drive successor selection.

The strongest current owner should be removed from consideration.

Recommendations should be generated from the remaining ownership distribution.

This approach is more robust to future ownership models.

---

## Observation 4

Successor recommendation is the first prescriptive capability in PIA.

Previous milestones focused on:

* Observation
* Estimation
* Risk Detection

This milestone focuses on:

* Suggested Action

---

## Observation 5

Current recommendations are expertise-based.

Future recommendation signals may include:

* Recent activity
* Review participation
* Expertise growth trends
* Documentation contributions
* Team structure
* Organizational hierarchy

---

## Key Insight

Knowledge concentration can be mitigated when successor candidates are identified before ownership loss occurs.

PIA now recommends mitigation paths rather than merely reporting organizational risks.
